Description of Africa

Africa is rising, Africa is brighter. Africa is on the march towards a more prosperous future in which all its citizens of all creeds and backgrounds empowered to realize their full potential, live with satisfaction and pride about their continent. A future with healthy, well educated people living in robust and developed economies.

The responsibility to build this continent rests with each and every African citezin especially young leaders, at continental level and within our countries; responsibility to ensure that decision makers adequately put priority where it is needed.

Statistics about Africa

With 60 percent of Africa’s population currently aged 24 and below, it is clear that Africa is a young continent. Acknowledging this fact, the leaders of the world decided to give a priority to the young population. This is translated into both the Agenda 2063 of the African Union, and the Agenda 2030 of the United Nations. As such, the call is emphasized on African governments to transform education and skills development, health and wellbeing, empowerment, employment and entrepreneurship, which lie at the heart of the march of Africa towards progress, sustainable peace and development.
